Thesis Project Summary

My master's thesis focuses on Data-Driven Patient Scheduling with AI and Reinforcement Learning, where I inferred decision-makers’ policies and patterns from historical data. It involves estimating the cost function from the expert's actions and predicting their future optimal actions. I developed skills in Quantitative Data Analytics, Machine Learning (ML), Reinforcement Learning (RL), Hypotheses Testing, Optimization, Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn, Predictive AI, Git, TensorFlow, and PyTorch.

1. What Problem I Solved

I developed and validated inverse Reinforcement Learning approaches (Riccati and Hamiltonian methods) to estimate key cost parameters (rejection and waiting costs) in hospital scheduling decisions. This enabled the reconstruction of decision-making processes from observed expert actions. To achieve this, I used algebraic equations, hypothesis testing, statistical power, and significance testing (α) to identify the underlying **Reinforcement Learning (RL) policy** of decision-makers.

2. Why This Problem Matters

Efficient patient scheduling is critical for reducing waiting times and minimizing rejections, directly impacting hospital efficiency and patient care. Understanding decision-making patterns allows for better resource allocation and operational improvements.

3. How My Results Lead to an Improvement

  • Scalability & Accuracy: The Riccati approach performed better for short time horizons, while the Hamiltonian approach was more effective for longer time horizons.
  • Reduction in Estimation Errors: Extending the time horizon improved cost parameter estimation accuracy.
  • Counterfactual Analysis: Using optimized parameters in an alternative scheduling model resulted in up to 62% cost reduction for one hospital and 32% for another, proving efficiency gains.

4. Skills I Gained

  • Optimization & Inverse Reinforcement Learning: Developed mathematical models to infer decision-making costs.
  • Statistical Analysis & Sensitivity Testing: Evaluated the robustness of cost estimations under different conditions.
  • Data Cleaning & Management: Processed hospital datasets containing 80+ columns by performing dimensionality reduction, merging redundant classes, and filtering out insignificant data using hypothesis testing.
  • Simulation & Data-Driven Modeling: Designed a simulation framework to generate scheduling decisions and assess model performance.
  • Python & Numerical Computing: Used Python, NumPy, and Scikit-learn for solving optimization problems and analyzing real hospital datasets.

Scalability Analysis

The **Hamiltonian approach** scales better as data increases, but its convergence is slower. When **data is under 500 days**, the **Riccati approach converges faster** due to its **quadratic cost function**, while for **larger datasets**, the Hamiltonian approach is preferable.
